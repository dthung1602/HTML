KOKINSHŪ XV: 791



While going somewhere, sunk in thought, Ise saw a burning field by the road and composed this. [1]

a) Kanji:

冬がれの
のべとわが身を
思ひせば
もえても春を
またまし物を		 b) Romanji:

Fuyugare no
nobe to wa ga mi wo
omohi seba
moete mo haru wo
matamasi mono wo		 c) English:

Withered in winter
Are the fields, and I-
Were I the same, then
Though I burn, the spring
Might be something to await. [2]
