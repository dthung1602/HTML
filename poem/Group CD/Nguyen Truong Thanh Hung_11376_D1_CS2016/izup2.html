IZUMI SHIKIBU

(?976-?)

HOMEPAGE BIOGRAPHY POET 1 > POET 2 POET 3


GOSHŪISHŪ IX: 509



On the way down to Izumi, one night she heard a miyakodori softly calling and composed this. [1]

a) Kanji:

言問はゞ
ありのまにまに
都鳥
みやこのことを
我に聞かせよ		 b) Romanji:

Kototo wa
ari no manimani
miyakodori
miyako no koto wo
ware ni kikaseyo		 c) English:

Should I ask,
Speak plainly,
O Capital bird,
And of the capital
Tell me. [2]


References:
